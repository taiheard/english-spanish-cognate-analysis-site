Hypothesis testing overview

The project employs independent samples t-tests, one-way ANOVA with Tukey post-hoc comparisons, and descriptive risk analysis. Each test is explained below alongside visual summaries.

Analysis 4: False friend rate by domain

Which domains deserve extra warning labels?

False friend rate (FFR) quantifies the percentage of deceptive pairs within each cultural domain.

  • High risk (≥ 10%): Family/Kinship, Emotions/Psychology, Food/Cuisine, Clothing/Appearance, Festivals.
  • Medium risk (5-10%): Values/Ethics, Housing, Economics, Time, Social.
  • Low risk (< 5%): Technology/Tools, Education, Government, Nature, Health, Religion, Arts.

Data quality & limitations

Transparency ensures findings remain credible and reproducible.

Completeness metrics

  • English & Spanish words: 100%
  • Relationship type: 100%
  • English attestation: 100%
  • Spanish attestation: 100%
  • Definitions: 69.8%

Known limitations

  • Temporal bias toward post-1800 corpora
  • Geographic emphasis on American/British English and Castilian Spanish
  • Register bias favoring written language
  • Binary loanword direction simplifies complex histories

Validation checks

  • Cross-source comparison of attestation dates (r = 0.73)
  • Manual review of flagged similarity outliers
  • LLM-generated content spot-checked at 88% accuracy
