From lag to synchrony

The chart reveals a clear convergence pattern: time gaps between English and Spanish word adoption narrowed dramatically over the centuries.

Pre-1600: gaps of ±200-600 years. Post-1800: near-zero gaps. Globalization reshaped lexical diffusion.
  • Many modern English tech words are repurposed Latin words (like sonar) that already existed in Spanish—but they still appear first in English due to technological innovation.
  • Since the 1980s, most technology terms appear first in English, reflecting America's dominance in digital innovation.
  • Modern technology terms show near-simultaneous adoption, with time gaps clustering tightly around zero.
